What your team gets

One place your staff open on day one — and keep using every day after.

📋 Protocols that stay current

Your SOPs live in the app. Update one, and every team member sees the new version and is reminded to re-read it. No more binders that are out of date the day they're printed.

🗺️ Training paths by role

A guided track for each role — assistant, associate doctor, scan tech — so a new hire always knows exactly what to learn next, week by week.

✅ Quizzes & sign-offs

Short quizzes confirm your team actually understood the material, and checkpoints let you sign off when they're ready — not just when they've clicked through.

🎥 Video check-offs

Staff record themselves doing a technique or script; you review and give feedback right in the app. Perfect for adjusting, scanning, and phone scripts.

🌅 Daily focus

A simple daily nudge keeps new habits front-of-mind during those crucial first weeks.

📊 Progress you can see

At a glance: who's on track, who's behind, who's ready to test out. No chasing people for updates.

03

Who runs the technical side

This is the part that makes it possible without an IT person.

Software like this normally needs a developer to set it up and keep it running. You don't have one — and you don't need one. Instead, an AI assistant (Claude) does that job for you.

You subscribe to Claude, point it at the training system, and simply tell it what you want in plain English, like:

💬 "Add our new assistant, Priya."

It sets up her account and training path.

💬 "Update our scan protocol."

It changes the SOP and re-notifies the team.

💬 "Put our logo and colors on it."

It handles the branding.

💬 "The video upload isn't working."

It looks into it and fixes it.

What this replaces

No developer to hire, no software company to wait on, no support tickets. Changes that would normally take a vendor days happen in minutes, on your schedule.

To be straight with you

The assistant is a tool you direct — it's not magic and it's not fully hands-off. You (or a manager) tell it what you want done, and it does the technical work behind the scenes. Think of it as a capable helper who never sleeps and never sends an invoice per task — not an employee who runs the practice for you.

05

The honest part

It's simple to run, but it isn't zero effort — and we'd rather set that expectation up front.

🛠️ A one-time setup

Getting started takes a few hours, mostly handled by your AI assistant. Your part is answering questions about your practice — your roles, your protocols, which videos to include.

⏱️ A little upkeep

Now and then you'll spend a few minutes telling the assistant what to change — a new hire, an updated protocol. Minutes, not hours; occasional, not daily.

👤 One owner

It works best when one person — you or a manager — "owns" it and keeps it current. Not a full-time job, but not nobody's job either.

🧠 Willing to use AI

You (or your owner) need to be comfortable typing requests to an AI assistant in plain English. If you can text, you can do this.
