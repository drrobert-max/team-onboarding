## The one thing to fix first

**Stop quoting the plan price.** Everything else on this call is coachable polish; this is the strategy itself. She had the RoF video teed up perfectly ("you'll get the exact price in a video before your first adjustment") and then said the number out loud anyway. The fix is literally to **stop talking after the RoF setup and bridge to the booking** — the 4-beat framework in the scorecard is the script.

## What to keep (this is real talent)

- Discovery and empathy are excellent — she builds trust fast and gets parents talking.
- The nervous-system explanation is clean and jargon-light.
- The $300→$67 anchor was delivered correctly.

## Coaching priorities (in order)

1. **Never say a plan number.** Replace the entire "$3–6k / pay-in-full / family discount" block with the RoF deflection (validate → personalize → sell the video → bridge to booking).
2. **Own the close.** After the exam frame, go straight to two specific times: *"I've got tomorrow at 4:15 or Thursday at 10 — which works better?"* Don't wait for the parent to ask.
3. **Collect the $67 on the call, every time.** When "I don't have my card," pivot: *"No problem — I can hold the slot with the card your husband has, or text you a secure link right now so you don't lose tomorrow's opening."* Don't let "I'll call back" end the call.
