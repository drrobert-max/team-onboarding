The close is fixable; the quote that made the recovery necessary is the problem.

## What to keep

- **The pinned callback.** Scheduled slot on her own calendar, named time, text confirmation. This is exactly what the Alonzo/Gianno and Jamie calls were missing — she demonstrably has this move now.
- The spouse-objection recovery (exam-first + dad-watches-the-video + re-secured commitment) — no un-booking this time.
- Collaborative measurable goal-setting, and the immediate paperwork link with the "don't open it until you're ready" nudge.

## Leak points to fix

1. **Same root cause as Ryker/Kion/Alonzo & Gianno: the price sheet is in the pitch.** This call shows the cost in the clearest way — the quote *created* the "steep"/husband objection she then had to spend five minutes defusing. Without the number, "discuss with my husband" likely never fires before a $67 decision.
2. **The mom asked to hear the number again — that was the escape hatch.** "I missed the exact number" could have been met with beat 2/3 of the framework (personalized plan, full finances in the RoF video) instead of a verbatim repeat.
3. **Goal math:** restating "twice a month" as "once a week, cut to twice a month" set a goal equal to the current state. Small, but goals are the yardstick the whole care plan gets judged by.
4. **Follow-up check for the owner:** did the 10 AM callback happen, and were the deposits taken for the Tuesday 7/28 4:15 double slot? Also note the mom's self-initiated interest in an adult evaluation — warm co-regulation lead.
