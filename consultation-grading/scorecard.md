# Consultation Call Scorecard & Framework — v2

Purpose: a repeatable, critical grading instrument for Becky's new-patient phone consultations. The job of this call is a sale: move a curious parent to a **booked exam that actually shows up** — without quoting the treatment-plan price. Every recording gets scored on the same 100-point rubric so we can trend performance and reverse-engineer a winning script.

Tone is locked: **soft urgency, direct mechanics.** Warmth on the emotional content, assumptive and specific on the booking mechanics. The promo ($67 today vs. the usual $300) carries the urgency, so she never applies emotional pressure.

---

## Deal economics & the strategy shift

- **What's sold on the call:** a paid new-patient exam, *"usually $300 → $67 today,"* deposit collected over the phone to hold the spot.
- **What is NOT quoted anymore:** the ongoing treatment-plan cost ($3,000–$6,000). Previously quoted on the consult; this is being revoked. Quoting a big number before any exam, results, or trust was the primary cause of the ~50% booking rate.
- **How the price question is now handled:** deflected to the **Report of Findings (RoF) video**. After the exam, the doctors build a detailed report — timeline, frequency, and finances — delivered as a video the family watches **at home, before returning to the office, before any commitment.**
- **Baseline → target:** consult-to-booking ~50% → **90%**. Exam-to-show ~70% → **90%**.

**Funnel warning:** removing the number doesn't delete the price objection, it moves it to the RoF stage. Booking and show rates should rise, but track **case acceptance after the RoF video** too — otherwise a booking-rate win can hide a downstream revenue leak.

**Deductions (flag every instance):**

- Deposit not collected on a booked call (-10)
- Accepted "I'll call back to schedule" without pinning a time (-10)
- Talked more than listened during discovery (-5)
- Answered an objection with a fact but never re-asked for the booking (-5 each)
- Jargon the parent clearly didn't follow, left unexplained (-3 each)

## Grade bands

- **90–100** — Closer. Bottle this call, it goes in the script.
- **75–89** — Strong, one or two leak points to fix.
- **60–74** — Booking may happen but leaving money on the table; show risk high.
- **Below 60** — Consultation is functioning as a friendly info line, not a sales conversation.

## The cost-question framework (the centerpiece skill)

When the parent asks "so how much is this going to cost me?" — and most will — the move is a 4-beat sequence. Never a number.

1. **Validate.** "That's the most important question you can ask, and I want you to get a real answer, not a guess." (Removes the feeling that she's dodging.)
2. **Reframe to personalization.** "Here's the honest truth: the plan is built completely around what your child's exam actually shows. Two kids the same age can need very different things. If I threw out a range right now, I'd either scare you with a number that's too high or set the wrong expectation with one that's too low. Neither is fair to you."
3. **Sell the RoF video as the benefit.** "So here's how we do it. After the exam, the doctors put together a detailed report of findings — the full picture, including the timeline, how often your child would come in, and exactly what the investment looks like. You get that as a video you watch at home, on your own time, before you ever step back in the office. You'll have every piece of information you need to make a decision, with zero pressure from us."
4. **Bridge back to the booking.** "So the exam is really the step that unlocks all those answers for you. I've got Tuesday at 3 or Thursday at 10 — which works better?"

**If pushed again** ("just give me a ballpark"): stay warm, don't cave.
> "I really get why you want a number — I'd want one too. But I'd be guessing, and I respect your decision too much to do that. That's exactly what the report of findings is for, and you'll have it before you owe us anything beyond today's exam. Let's get your child in and get you those real answers."

**What earns full marks on Dimension 1:** validated the question, never gave a number, reframed to personalization, sold the RoF as a benefit (at-home, no-pressure, full-info-before-commitment), bridged back to the booking, and held the line without getting defensive if pushed.

**What loses points:** a flat "I can't tell you that," any hint of a number, sounding annoyed or cornered, or deflecting but then failing to re-ask for the booking.

## Ideal call flow (v2)

1. **Open & frame** — warm greeting, names, set the agenda, take control.
2. **Discover** — the child, the struggle, what they've tried, the family toll. Ask, then listen.
3. **Bridge** — reflect back, connect to what the exam uncovers.
4. **Frame the exam** — what it is, why it matters for a child like theirs, that it's the gateway to their personalized answers.
5. **Ask** — assumptive booking, two specific times.
6. **Handle the cost question** — the 4-beat framework above. This will usually fire here or during the ask.
7. **Handle other objections** — acknowledge, reframe, re-ask.
8. **Lock the show** — collect the $67 deposit, confirm, set expectations and reminders.
9. **Close warm** — reassure, name what happens next (exam → RoF video at home), end confident.
